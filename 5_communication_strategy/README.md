# Communication Strategy  
**Target Audience:** African Union – Continental Investment Promotion & Policy Leaders  

---

## Purpose of This Communication Strategy

The purpose of this communication strategy is to translate the technical research findings into **actionable**, **accessible**, and **policy-relevant** insights for African decision-makers shaping the continent’s investment environment.

This includes policymakers at:

- **African Union Commission (AUC)** — Economic Development, Trade, Industry & Mining (ETIM)  
- **Regional Economic Communities (RECs)** — ECOWAS, EAC, SADC, COMESA  
- **National Investment Promotion Agencies (IPAs)**  
- **Infrastructure, transport, energy, and ICT ministries**  
- **Pan-African development partners** (AfDB, UNECA, AUDA-NEPAD)

The strategy ensures that research messages are communicated **clearly**, **persuasively**, and in formats **optimized for policy uptake**.

---

## Communication Goals

- Inform AU policymakers about the **strongest determinants of FDI** in developing economies.  
- Shift the policy narrative away from **overemphasizing governance reforms** and toward an **infrastructure-led investment strategy**.  
- Provide actionable recommendations aligned with **Agenda 2063** (“A prosperous Africa based on inclusive growth and sustainable development”).  
- Support **evidence-based decision-making** in FDI policy, infrastructure prioritization, and investor retention programs.  

---

## Target Audience Characteristics

### **Primary Audience**
**African Union policymakers** responsible for investment, trade, infrastructure, industrialization, and economic integration.

**Attributes:**
- High-level decision-makers  
- Limited time for technical detail  
- Prioritize **strategic clarity**, **fiscal feasibility**, **political relevance**  
- Prefer communication that is **concise**, **visually clear**, and **implementable**  

### **Secondary Audience**
- Investment Promotion Agencies (IPAs)  
- Ministries of Energy, Transport, ICT, Trade, Finance  
- Development Finance Institutions (DFIs)  
- Economic advisors and research departments  
- Pan-African think tanks

---

## Communication Formats

### **1. Policy Brief (Primary Output)**
A 2–3 page minister-level brief including:
- Key findings  
- Why they matter for Africa  
- Practical steps for policymakers  
- Infrastructure & investment priority map  

**Tone:** authoritative, concise, evidence-based.

---

### **2. Visual Storytelling Assets**
- Tableau dashboards  
- Choropleth map of FDI trends  
- Random Forest feature importance diagrams  
- Infrastructure → FDI causal diagrams  

**Purpose:** help policymakers *see* relationships rather than infer them.

---

### **3. Slide Deck for AU Ministers**
A short, high-impact presentation for:
- AU Ministerial Council meetings  
- REC-level industrialization summits  
- IPA annual conferences  

---

### **4. One-Page Infographic**
Designed for:
- Social media  
- AU newsletters  
- Stakeholder dissemination  

Summarizes the core insights on one visual page.

---

## Core Policy Messages (Key Narrative)

### **Message 1—FDI Is Sticky**
Investment follows existing investment.  
**Implication:** Investor retention must be a top AU priority.

---

### **Message 2—Infrastructure Outperforms Governance**
Electricity access and logistics quality are **stronger predictors** of FDI than corruption indicators.  
**Implication:** Infrastructure investment yields the highest returns.

---

### **Message 3—Africa Must Prepare for Global Cycles**
Global market conditions exert major influence on FDI flows.  
**Implication:** Countries need **fiscal buffers** and **countercyclical tools**.

---

### **Message 4—The AU Can Shift the Continent’s Trajectory**
Continental coordination can:
- Increase Africa’s bargaining power  
- Harmonize investment policies  
- Streamline reforms  
- Reduce inter-country competition for incentives  

**Implication:** The AU is uniquely positioned to lead an infrastructure-first investment strategy.
